Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics – AI Systems in the Physical World

Welcome to the comprehensive textbook on Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. This book bridges artificial intelligence and robotics, providing a thorough understanding of how intelligent systems can be embodied in physical form.

About This Textbook​

This textbook is organized in 2 comprehensive parts with 20 detailed chapters that take you from basic robotics concepts to advanced autonomous humanoid systems. Each chapter includes practical examples, exercises, and real-world applications.

Structure​

Part I – Foundations & Robotic Systems​

  1. What Is Physical AI? - Introduction to Physical AI concepts
  2. Basics of Robotics Systems - Fundamental robotics components
  3. AI Meets the Physical World - Bridging AI and physical systems
  4. Software Stack for Humanoid Robots - Robotics software architecture
  5. Simulation Before Reality - Simulation and sim-to-real transfer
  6. Introduction to ROS 2 - ROS 2 fundamentals
  7. Nodes, Topics, and Messages - ROS 2 communication
  8. Services, Actions, and Parameters - Advanced ROS 2 communication
  9. Writing ROS 2 Nodes with Python (rclpy) - Python ROS 2 development
  10. URDF & Robot Description - Robot modeling and description

Part II – Digital Twins, Autonomy & Vision-Language-Action​

  1. Gazebo: Physics & Simulation - Physics simulation environment
  2. Sensors in Simulation - Sensor simulation techniques
  3. Unity for Human-Robot Interaction - Unity robotics integration
  4. NVIDIA Isaac Sim - Advanced simulation platform
  5. Isaac ROS & Navigation (Nav2) - GPU-accelerated robotics
  6. What Is Vision-Language-Action (VLA)? - Multimodal AI systems
  7. Voice to Text with Whisper - Speech recognition integration
  8. Language to Action Planning - Natural language to robot commands
  9. Autonomous Decision Making - Autonomous systems architecture
  10. Capstone: The Autonomous Humanoid - Complete system integration

Target Audience​

This textbook is designed for:

  • O/A Level students learning robotics and AI
  • Engineering students learning robotics and AI
  • Medical & Science students learning robotics and AI
  • Beginner to intermediate AI practitioners
  • Developers working with ROS 2 and autonomous systems
  • Researchers exploring Physical AI and humanoid robotics
  • Practitioners implementing robotics solutions

Getting Started​

Begin with Part I, Chapter 1: What Is Physical AI? to start your journey into Physical AI and robotics. Each chapter builds upon previous knowledge, creating a comprehensive learning experience.

Learning Philosophy​

This textbook follows a progressive learning approach from concepts → systems → autonomy. It emphasizes simulation-first development, hardware-agnostic design, and safety-aware implementation.

Features​

  • Comprehensive coverage of ROS 2 and robotics development
  • Integration with modern simulation platforms (Gazebo, Isaac Sim)
  • Vision-Language-Action (VLA) systems implementation
  • NVIDIA Isaac ROS integration
  • Beginner-friendly with practical examples
  • Industry-aligned content and practices
